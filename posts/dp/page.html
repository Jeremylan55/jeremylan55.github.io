DinoPanic

DESIGN BRIEF

Mobile gaming has exploded the past few years. My interest in this field grew while I was getting more exposure to the latter. This project serve as an learning and experimental excercise about games designed especially for mobile devices. Those games often rely on very simple interactions to let users play. The objective was to design a "one tap" game from start to finish and pick up as much asknowledge as possible on the way.

Player motives

Players that would be targeted are casual mobile gamers. Those players often play when they have small gaps in their busy agenda. Their free time is often scarce, They need to be able to quickly start a session and end it. Often they will start playing in public transit because it is one of the few times of the day that they are free.

This brings up the need to design a game that is very simple and highly comfortable to play with one hand in shaky environments such as the subway. This is where knowing the most accessible touch areas is very important. The picture below describes the most comfortable areas in green. So we will be expecting the player to touch the screen in those green areas.

Touch screen heat map

Furthermore, the hand of many of our users may cover part of the screen. It is important to take into consideration this factor while laying out the visuals.

PLAYER FLOW

Flow diagram

Players need to be able to play again with minimal navigation. The chosen design consist of a closed loop that navigates between the result screen and the game scene. The player can play indefinitely while using only the simplest interaction possible which is tapping on the screen.

CHARACTER DESIGN

Characters silhouette test

Several designs of the character were made. The one with the most distinguishable silhouette was chosen. To help immerse our players in the game, the character needed to seem alive. The most effective and clear way to express the sense of life was to make the character go through three stages of life. The being birth, living and then death. Below you can see the models of those 3 stages

Character stages

SCENE DESIGN

The screen needed to communicate to the players.

  • The medium

    From the start, once the player taps the screen, the character is born and start moving. This tells the player that tapping on screen cause the character to move. Moving is the medium.

  • The obstacles.

    The player can immediately see that the part of the ground is lava denoted in orange. The same visual style is used to represent the falling meteor so that the player can identify the latter as an obstacle.

  • The game objective

    The objective is communicated by having the fruits to be of the opposite colour to the obstacles.
