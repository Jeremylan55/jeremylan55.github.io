PIGGYSAVE

DESIGN BRIEF

REDLab is the innovation lab of Manulife found at Communitech. The REDLab's duty is to explore the possible applications of emerging technologies to improve financial services. Every sprint the lab designs and develops a solution to a problem faced by clients.

Brainstorming section and low-fi designs for this project at "REDLab"

One sprint I decided to tackle the issue that, many new parents of the early 2000's have the belief that their children are too frivolous and do not understand the value of money. Though a diary study among several parents, we found out that many children are not aware how to save because their parent rarely trusted them with money. The children are also easily tempted to spend their money impulsively. We explored how gaming and constant encouragement will help to change the children's habits and build trust with their parents.

Personas

The challenge we faced was to have access to the demography we were interested in. We performed interviews at libraries and places where families can be found. After 50+ interviews, we defined our user's personas below.

User personas

MOOD SHEET

I identified popular games and educational apps for 7-10 years olds. Below are a mood sheet and interaction templates common in those apps. We found that UI components are very large to make it accessible for the children. Also, made affordance of the buttons very clear by making them very responsive. We found that most of the gameplay was revolved around an avatar.

Mood sheet

GAME FLOW

The main challenge was to make the saving process for children stimulating. The gameplay would encourage them at milestones by awarding with items that they can use to customise their piggy bank. Providing rewards will stimulate them to continue toward their goal.

One key feature we introduced was the ability for parents to give "Virtual" money to their children and rewarding them with the actual funds when they reach their goals. This would create trust between both users.

Game flow

Characters

The game needed an avatar for the players. A piggy bank was chosen as the avatar because children's easily associate it with saving money.

Below are tentative sketches and different kind of avatar ,the solution could use. We took heavy inspiration from "Alphabear" to make the piggy bank. The cube shape enabled it to be customisable.
