Overview

During my time at Shopify, I got the opportunity to work with the Home team on the admin dashboard. I was in charge of several aspects of the Home page from redesigning key UI elements to implementing new ones. I also led stakeholder meetings to clearly communicate the design goals and specifications to PMs, developers, content strategists and designers, making sure the projects were aligned with overall strategy and product direction. I got the opportunity work extensively on the visual design, product design, information architecture and content strategy of Home.

Understanding Merchant Goals

Every day half a million merchants go to their home page to plan their day. Some of those merchants have full-time jobs and have very little to spare on their store while others consider their shop as the one and only source of revenue. Those merchants have very specific needs. They are definitely not your common social media users! They look for tangible content that will impact their store.
Home delivers this content through actionable cards that that are ranked by a recommender system.
The following design principles help Home to always benefit the merchants.

  • Home guides where work is to be done, work is not done on Home.
  • Guide and teach the merchant on the best course of action.

Home Notifications

Goals

  • Alert user of important issues concerning the state of their store.
  • Clearly explain the type and urgency of the issue.
  • Be able to dismiss notifications where it is possible to clear the feed.
  • Make the merchant aware of the consequences.

Information Architecture

Not every notification deal with the same type of issue, some issues can be only informative while others might be very urgent like a payment transaction failing. The notifications can be organised into different tiers and escalate to another higher tier if necessary.

Results

A/B test showed that 14% of the users closed irrelevant and resolved notifiactions. This helped in reducing the number of support tickets.

Home Blog Cards Redesign

Reading through user feedback, I noticed that the blog cards were polarising among the users, some really learned a lot from the articles while others found them very distracting. Word count pointed out that 45% of those that had negative opinions on the matter, though that they were "spams".

Goals

  • Show value and time investment in reading the blog.
  • Make blog cards less distracting to the workflow of merchants.
  • Convey trustworthiness of content.

Final Design

The idea or reduction of redundant content was kept from the previous version and it was chosen that only 2 articles would be shown in the feed by the recommender system.
The final design polished the drawback of the previous version by using illustrations to increase scanability and while the content was, even more, streamline to only 3 components. First, one is a reason why we show this to the merchant to increase trust in the recommender system. The second piece of content would be a curated title specially for the card as sometime article would have too long titles. The third piece of content is the amount of time needed to be invested by the busy merchant reading the article.
