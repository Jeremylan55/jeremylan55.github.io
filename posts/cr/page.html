Cirro by Boltmade

Overview

Fall 2016 was spent at Boltmade (acquired by shopify), a consulting firm that specializes in software design and development. Everything at boltmade is vertically integrated, boltmade helps small startups to large multinationals to build their product from scratch or add value to their service through design thinking.

My roles at boltmade involved designing features, creating the visual identity of clients products and performing user research/testing to make sure that the product succeeds.

The "Boltmade" office prior to acquisition by "Shopify"

Cirro is a B2B and SaaS mobile flight management system for fixed wing and helicopter operators that automate the compliance process of flight planning.

Design goals

My job as a designer at Boltmade was to transform the design of the MVP into a robust, scalable and adaptive product for mobile tablet and desktop.

To create consistent experience across platforms

To reach the largest market possible and work on the different platforms the operators uses Cirro 2.0 was designed to be adaptive to multiple screens and hardware. E.g: phone, tablet, desktop.

To create patterns easily recognizable by the users

Display relevant information for pilots that are critical at each stage in the user flow. Red routes were defined at the project kick-off so that to prioritize where to improve the usability of the product. For examples: Creating a new flight report is directly available on the dashboard or 3 clicks away from anywhere in the software.

To make the product completely touch and click friendly.

The UI components were designed with the human factor in mind from the beginning. E.g : Buttons are at least 10mm on screen and reducing mouse travel on desktop.

Research

It was crucial for to identify the target users and experience their daily routine by visiting a few small to medium aircraft operators. There I interviewed multiple employees ranging from Administrators, Pilots, field engineers to executives and perform a diary study to determine their needs and frustrations.

Visiting the operators was crucial in understanding their needs

Arm with all the knowledge I was able to brainstorm with the team to produce several potential user flows. Those proposals went through a rigorous selection by Boltmade and the client.The chosen one was used for the next stage of the design process

I used the atomic design principle to build the UI components of Cirro. I started by defunding the Typography, text fields and buttons to make sure that redesign was extremely structured and provided the building blocks for future features. Those parts were assembled to create different components which enable a wide variety of actions but remain familiar to the users.

Atomic design principle use to create the components for "Cirro"

TESTING

A critical part was to ensure that the product worked as predicted and is a joy for users to use. I crafted the wireframes using "Sketch" and "Pixate" to seek initial feedback internally and eventually moved to test it with a few pilots. For this process, we mostly used A/B testing strategy to determine the best solution.

Countless wireframes were used for A/B testing

After a dozen round of one to one interviews, I compiled and prioritized the list of discrepancies based whether they were on critical route or had a low SUS score. Due to the limited amount of time that I had the prioritization of task helped me deliver in a timely manner.

Prioritised list of Usability issues to be fixed with current system
