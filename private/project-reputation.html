Project Overview

Marvel contest of champion is a mobile game from Kabam with a massive player base.
This evergreen title is looking for to keep their end game players entertained. This project involves using UX methodologies and lean UX to prototype and validate Ideas quickly.

  • Product
    Mobile game
  • Time frame
     16 weeks
  • Deliverables
    User research, Interviews, User flows, User testing, Sketches, Wireframes, Mockups, Project management
  • Team composition
    1 UI UX designer, 1 Game designer

Problem

As MCoC grew to become and evergreen title so did the need to keep players engaged on the long term. After completing the end game players look out for seasonal content to keep them engaged. However, this seasonal content is often tailor made and cannot be delivered on a day-to-day basis. Players often seek more interactive ways such as Alliances. These Alliances have become the pillar of our endgame experience that keep players coming back day after day for years. However, these Alliances are running short on how they can keep our players stimulated with new unique moments.
‍

Objectives

Design a solution that would engage end game players and keep them playing after finishing the campaign

Keeping End game players engaged

The team sought to explore a new progression system that would be always challenging to high level players. This solution would enable players to be constantly challenged by having them compete against each other in the form of a small local ranking segmented into tiers.
‍

Who are we serving?

Working with game design and UXR, I helped design a questionnaire and listen to interviews to understand who our end game players are. This helped me define the player Archetypes.

player goals and the potential solutions

At this point the pandemic hit, and we had to adapt. I held in person/online workshops with stakeholders and team members so help us empathize with the players.
We used the Jobs To Be Done framework to define the goals of these endgame players. These workshops were held online using Mirro and Lucid chart. I used the How Might We framework to enable the team to jam on ideas and have everyone from Game design & UX to have a say.

Understanding players' needs at each touch points

Base on Game design the experience would be massive and with many touch points.
I created user journeys maps to help the team change from viewing the experience on screen by screen basis and to a more heuristically. This enabled us to see all the friction points in the experience and plan how to solve them.

Ideation

I was personally in charge of the level up experience and create wireframes to make the ideas more tangible and enable the team to see the feature with minimal effort beforehand and decide about the direction.

UI art

I followed the art style of the game to bring the wireframe to life and created the concept for the badges for the levels. I created storyboards to define the level up sequence.
