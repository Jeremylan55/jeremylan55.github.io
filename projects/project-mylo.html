Mylo App
Bank linking & more

Project Overview

Mylo is a fintech who aspired to help Canadians to achieve their financial goals by making investing accessible to everyone through a mobile app with 750 000 downloads so far.
They needed UX help to relaunch the app to have better conversion rate to secure their first Series A funding. I got the opportunity to work closely with people such as the Product lead, CMO and CFO. I got the opportunity to work on several features for the app notably: Onboarding, TFSA /RRSP, Transfers, bank linking.

  • Product
    Mobile App
  • Deliverables
    User flows, Sketches, Wireframes, Mockups, Branding, Design system
  • Tools
    Pen & Paper, SketchApp,  Abstract

Problem

Most Mylo users have their bank connected from the time they sign-in but some of them don't because Mylo could not verify their detail automatically or their bank gets sporadically disconnected because their bank is requesting their login credentials again. For those who experience that it can be very frustrating.
This results in users often lashing out on review boards and contacting customer support excessively.

Objectives

My objectives were to design a better experience for users to connect their bank account and keep them connected to the app. Thus increasing customer satisfaction and number of activations.

  • Time to Completion
  • System Usability Scale (SUS)
  • Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

Preliminary research

I initially started by interviewing the customer success agents at the company to build an understanding of the problems. These were the most common types of requests related to bank issues.

The main reasons these users were experiencing these issues is primarily because Mylo legally requires the user's identity and financial institutions to be verified. Additionally banks would required users to login or answer security questions sporadically for security reasons.
Based on the legal requirements provided by the company’s CFO/financial expert, I built a list of “jobs” for the user.

User journeys

I created 3 user journeys for the 3 main JTBDs so that I could find the pain points and opportunities for our users. I interviewed a few users who had when through the current process successfully. The main pain points that were observed is that users don’t really understand what is happening and what they have to do when these errors happening which result in them contacting customer success which was at this time the only way to solve these errors

Key findings

Bank statement -  Users were unaware of what document it was and where to find it. I added more context and information about the bank statement.

Final design results

Streamline way to upload documents

It is easy for users to know at a glance what document they need to upload. Additionally they can upload them separately whenever they have time.

Automatically alert users when they have to reconnect

No need to wait or try to figure out why bank accounts are not connected. Mylo will alert the user when they need to reconnect their accounts.

Take picture of your documents

No more need to create PDF and email it to customer success. Users can now take pictures of their document for verification.
