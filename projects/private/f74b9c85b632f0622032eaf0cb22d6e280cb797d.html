Kabam Sparx 2.0
Content management for games

Project Overview

Kabam’s constantly growing portfolio of games resulted in an ever-growing cost to maintain these games. With 2 new titles plan to launch the need to revamp their content creation pipeline was its highest.
Sparx is a web based application that enable live-ops, QA, Business Analysts and Engineers to create content and maintain games for over 200 million players.
My responsibilities for this project was to redesign from the ground up the offer creation process for Kabam and prove user-centered methods can be applied to remaining areas of the tool.

  • Product
    Web, Service design
  • Time frame
     10 weeks
  • Deliverables
    User research, Interviews, User flows, User testing, Sketches, Wireframes, Mockups, Project management
  • Team composition
    1 UI UX designer, 3 Software engineers, Several live-ops Analyst

Problem

The current web tool has not evolved since 2006, resulting in a clunky process with obscure workflows that are irrelevant to today’s needs which are resulting in a lot of human hours being wasted navigating and learning the current tool. Consequently, this prevents the employees to take on more challenging and impactful tasks as most of their time is spent fighting the tool.

Objectives

  • Efficient - Work can be completed faster, freeing people’s time to achieve more and doing more with less people
  • Agnostic - The tool capabilities are universal and meaningful across contexts. 
  • Scalable - Sparx handles the operational growth, servicing an increasing number of products and staff.
  • Time to Completion
  • System Usability Scale (SUS)
  • Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

Process

For this project, the team used the UX sprint process to make sure that we would achieve our deliverables. Parkinson’s Law states that tasks will inflate with infinite time. The process was divided into these main stages: Defining the problem and scope, User research, Define goals and KPIs Prototyping & testing, evaluation

Preliminary research

Diary study

The goal of the user research was to have a deeper understanding of the current workflow and the pain points of the users. Interviews were conducted with a set of users ranging from senior/highly experienced employees to more junior employees. Having these 2 types of user help us understand the process from a user who is used to the current process and users that are new to the process. Surveys were sent out to identify the severity of the problem.

Who are the users?

88 users were interviewed from different roles in the company. These people were involved in the offer creation process at the company. A user satisfaction survey was sent to have a preliminary understanding of who we were designing for.

Information architecture

The main challenge is to create a tool that provides a new workflow that is more effective but yet familiar to the user. For that, I used tree sorting exercises to test the potential IA of the page and pick the one we believed matched the expectation of users the most.

Testing the design

Usability testing

Participants were gathered and online session was done to test the prototype. Usability issues were prioritised between, Critical: User can't finish task, Major: user take considerate amount of time and need guidance to finish task and Minor: Issues that do not cause any of the former problems but affect the user expereience.

Key findings

Many users had issues with how to build a user condition to target a specific user. Many of them were not programmers and they could not understand the programmer mental models. After some interviews, I created new designs based on their mental models and ran a survey among the users which result in a significant increase in readability.The design tried to speed up workflow by filling in details by default to reduce the number of steps. The users did not understand why certain sections were being filled automatically and were disoriented. The design changed by hiding duplicated content to help with redundancy.

Final design results

All In one go

Live-Ops Analysts can complete the offer creation process on 1 page. V1 of Sparx required users to navigate in and out of multiple pages which required a lot of cognitive load.

Searchable content

User can type to quickly get, Item such as existing offers, redeemers and player conditions without needing to know exactly how they named or in which category they are nested. V1 of Sparx was overly complex, with obscured information architecture that prevented people to find where things are.

Scalable design

By using Atomic design principles and reusable components, New features can be build quickly while user don't have to lean new interaction patterns.

Key takeaways

  • Always keep moving
     Based on Pareto’s principle, 80% of impact is done in 20% of the time. I can get absorbed by the minute details when designing such a complex feature but I had to always look at the heuristic experience and goals of the design.
  • Involve key stakeholders early
    UX design in a team sport. There are a lot of moving parts in an organization that is growing as fast a Kabam. It is important to get feedback from engineers to keep them in the loop.
  • Design for flexibility
    As a designer, I might have a specific idea of how the optimal experience will be but when doing these usability tests, I will quickly realize that most users won’t use the product like what I intended. E.g: design for keyboard shortcuts and being able to navigate the form without a mouse.
