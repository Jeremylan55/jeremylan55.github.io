Petal
Home energy monitor

Project Overview

For my final year design project, my team decided to tackle the global energy problem on a smaller scale where anyone can make an impact on their life. The project lasted for less than 12 weeks where we had to build a fully functional prototype to test our concept.
Petal is a home energy monitor that can be retrofitted in any home and apartment without the need of an electrician. Users can track their electricity usage and identify areas in their home that are consuming the highest amount of energy. The product hopes to help them be more conscious and save energy as a result.

Problem

Energy conscious homeowners and families are willing to be more eco-friendly, waste less energy and wish to save on their electricity bill. However, they do not have the ability to identify specific areas where energy is being wasted in their home or build good usage habits without costly initial cost and big changes in their habits
Based on our survey, 88% of them don’t know don’t know the cost of their electricity before the end of the month. The average home electricity bill will be $215 in 2028 based on a prediction from the CBC.

Objectives

Design and build a solution that will help users to be more aware of their electricity usage and save on their bills.

Process

The project had a lot of deliverables such, UI/UX design, Industrial design & Electrical engineering. To achieve these deliverables in 24 weeks, we used the MVP process. This would enable us to set quality assurance and reduce development time.

Preliminary research

Our initial research goal was to identify if there was an audience for the product and build and insight on their pain points. I use "Typeform" to survey 25 people from friends and family. This helped us have an insight about the problem.

There were common pain points that most people we interview had. Most of them were only aware of their electricity usage at the end of the month. Users don’t know when are On-peak/Off-peak hours. People has difficulties understanding their usage from their paper bills.

Problem framing

The problem was pretty broad and as a team we needed to narrow down out problem space to be able to solve it effectively. I held some workshops with the team to get all of us aligned on what we were trying to solve.

  • Who
    - People who are conscious of power consumption
    - Home owners
  • What
    - Electricity monitoring
    - Paying for the current hour, peaks and low rates.
  • Why
    - Electricity cost is at an all time high
    - Demand for electricity is increasing
    - Bad for environment
  • Where
    - Ontario homes, where electricity is the most expensive

Competitive analysis

I did analysis of competitive products that are helping users to save right in their home. They rage from the least intrusive to the most intrusive. The main barrier of entry for our users were technical skills and initial investments. Having to call in an electrician to install the device or needing to tinker with high power wires was the biggest challenge that our users could have.
Smart plug have very low initial cost but they can only give users a very small insight of only a few appliances which might not help users save a lot. On the total end of the spectrum home batteries require a lot of effort and initial cost to install but they are really good at saving users money. 
We decided to go with a smart meter that could be installed without needing to do any rewiring and that would be able to scan all appliances in the entire house.
